# CRE Document Standards - build spec for the Holiner tools

Researched standard structure and verbiage for each document type the tools produce, anchored to
Texas / DFW commercial practice. This is the spec the document builders are aligned to.

> **Two legal caveats that shape the build:**
> 1. **TREC does not promulgate commercial forms.** Commercial listing, representation, and commission
>    agreements in Texas come from trade associations (Texas REALTORS / TAR) or attorneys - and the **TAR
>    commercial forms are copyrighted and member-licensed**, so the tools generate *original standard-style
>    language*, not TAR verbatim.
> 2. **Non-binding vs. binding.** LOIs and Offers to Purchase are non-binding term sheets - the tools can
>    fully generate these. The binding agreements (representation, listing, commission) are where Holiner
>    likely uses its TAR member forms; the tool can produce a standard-language draft and/or capture the
>    data to transfer onto the official form. **Any generated document should get an attorney/broker review
>    before client use.**

---

## 1. Letter of Intent - Lease (non-binding term sheet)
Short term sheet that captures deal economics before the lease is drafted. One to a few pages.

**Standard sections:** Parties (legal names) · Property (address, type, RSF/USF, suite) · Permitted Use ·
Lease Term (commencement, rent-commencement, delivery dates; expiration) · Renewal / Extension Options
(notice period, renewal rate) · Base Rent (amount + annual escalations) · Lease Structure (**NNN / Gross /
Modified Gross** - who pays taxes, insurance, CAM/opex) · Additional Rent / Operating Expenses · Security
Deposit (amount, form - cash or LOC) · Concessions (TI allowance, free-rent/abatement) · Delivery Condition
· Parking · Signage · Assignment / Subletting · Maintenance & Repairs · Exclusivity / Co-tenancy (retail) ·
Contingencies (zoning, financing, board/franchisor approval) · **Binding provisions called out**
(Confidentiality, Exclusivity/no-shop, Access) · Non-binding disclaimer · Broker disclosure · Expiration of
the LOI.

---

## 2. Letter of Intent / Offer to Purchase (non-binding term sheet)
Term sheet for an acquisition, ahead of the Purchase & Sale Agreement (PSA).

**Standard sections:** Parties (Buyer/Seller legal names) · Property (address, legal description, land/asset
type) · Purchase Price · Earnest Money / Deposit (**typically 1-5% of price**; refundable → non-refundable
after due diligence; held in escrow) · Due Diligence / Inspection Period (**30-90 days**; environmental,
zoning, title, survey, property condition) · Financing terms/contingency · Title & Survey · Seller
Representations & Warranties · Closing Date (**30-90 days**) · Closing costs allocation · Confidentiality ·
Exclusivity / no-shop period · Broker/commission disclosure · Non-binding disclaimer · Expiration of the offer.

---

## 3. Commercial Listing Agreement - Exclusive Right to Sell / Lease (binding; landlord/seller rep)
Binding engagement of Holiner by the owner. In TX this is a TAR/association or attorney form (not TREC).

**Standard sections:** Parties · Property · **Grant of exclusive right** ("Seller appoints Broker as
Seller's sole and exclusive agent and grants the exclusive right to market and sell/lease the Property") ·
Listing term (start/end) · Listing price / lease terms sought · **Broker compensation** (% of sale price or
lease value; when earned - at closing / lease execution) · **Seller obligations** (cooperate with showings;
refer all inquiries to Broker; do not list with another broker during the term) · **Protection period**
("within [10] days after the Listing ends Broker may deliver written notice of named prospects; if Seller
transacts with a named prospect during the protection period, the fee is owed") · Marketing authorization ·
Intermediary / disclosed-dual-agency notice · Representations · Default & remedies · Mediation / dispute
resolution · Entire agreement; written modification only; no assignment without consent; joint & several
liability; **Texas law governs** · Signatures.

---

## 4. Buyer / Tenant Representation Agreement - Exclusive (binding; buyer/tenant rep)
Binding engagement of Holiner by the buyer/tenant. TAR commercial buyer/tenant representation form in TX.
*(Note: as of 2026, written representation agreements are required in more Texas situations.)*

**Standard sections:** Parties (Client, Broker) · **Grant of exclusive right** ("Client grants Broker the
exclusive right to act as Client's agent to acquire property in the market area") · Definitions ("Acquire" =
purchase or lease; "Closing" = title conveyance (sale) or binding lease (lease)) · Market area & property
type · Term · **Broker compensation** (who pays - typically landlord/seller-paid; how calculated; when
earned) · **Client representations** (not party to another rep agreement; information is true) ·
Intermediary status (if Client pursues one of Broker's listings) · Confidentiality · Default · Escrow
authorization · Mediation · Entire agreement; Texas law · Signatures.

---

## 5. Commission Agreement (binding)
Standalone confirmation of the fee, often used broker-to-broker or with an owner outside a full listing.

**Standard sections:** Parties · Property / transaction identified · **Commission structure** (% of sale
price or lease value, or flat fee; tiered if applicable) · **When earned & payable** (paid by seller/lessor;
sale → at closing; lease → at lease execution; split schedule e.g. halves) · Protection / registration of
named prospect · Conditions (fee still owed if seller defaults on an executed contract or cancels by
agreement with a registered prospect) · Compensation is negotiable / not set by any association ·
Dispute resolution · Texas law · Signatures. *(This is the money side that feeds the invoice.)*

---

## 6. Client Intake / Requirement Builder (internal needs assessment)
Not a legal document - the broker's structured capture of a client's requirement, which then seeds the LOI /
offer / CRM.

**Standard fields:** Company & contacts · Representation side (tenant/buyer) · **Space size** (RSF range;
sizing by headcount ~125-175 SF/employee for office) · **Asset type / use** · **Location / submarket**
(preferred areas, traffic/car counts & demographics for retail, drive-time) · Parking needs (ratio,
reserved) · **Budget** (rate/SF or total; NNN vs gross expectation) · **Timeline** (target occupancy, lease
expiry / must-move date, term length desired) · Growth / expansion outlook · Build-out / TI needs ·
Special requirements (power, HVAC, loading, medical/industrial specifics) · Financing / approvals · Decision
process & stakeholders · Notes.
